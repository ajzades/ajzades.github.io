About me

Why GIS?

Well, what a great question. I guess my respone would be, why not! As someone who loves the outdoors (except for winter, as we now know) I couldn't think of a better field for myself.
Upon taking my first introduction course, I really felt like I finally found a career path that I am interested in. My natural curiosity led me to a major in geography and my love for nature and desire to preserve it is what brought me to sustainability, but GIS is something else all together.
To be frank, I didn't know what GIS was just two years ago. The words raster and vector were foreign to me. That being said, in just two short years I have really felt my skills and knowledge develop.
My favorite part about GIS is all the learning involved. I mean, last week I didn't know the first thing about coding, and now I'm building a website...WHAT! How cool!? GIS has really opened another door into my brain that I didn't know existed. So I guess, that's why GIS!

A recipe inspired by British Bake Off: Shortbread Biscuits

As we know, I don't ski and I hate winter. So what better way to embrace this upcoming holiday season, than a recipe from me to you.

  • 125g salted butter
  • 55g caster sugar
  • 200 g plain flour

steps

  1. Put the butter and sugar in the bowl of a food processor and mix until combined. Add the flour and pulse briefly until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Tip out the mixture onto a work surface and gently bring it together, then knead it into a dough. Once the dough is formed, wrap it in cling film and chill for 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees f
  3. Once the dough has chilled, lightly flour the work surface and roll out the dough until it is about the thickness of a penny. Use cookie cutters to stamp out cookies.
  4. Sprinkle the biscuits with a little caster sugar and bake for 10â€“12 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  5. Let the cookies cool for ten minutes on the baking sheet then move to a cooling rack. Eat!
